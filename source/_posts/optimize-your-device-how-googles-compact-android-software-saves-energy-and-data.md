##  Why Use "Lite" Apps?

 The cool thing is you don't need to have an Android Go device to download the Go apps. Anyone can download them from the Play Store and get a lighter Google experience. But why would you want to intentionally use apps with fewer features than their full-size counterparts?

 First and foremost, the Go apps are smaller in size. For example, the full [Google Maps](https://www.anrdoezrs.net/links/3607085/type/dlg/sid/UUhtgUeUpU207252/https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.apps.maps) app uses about 270MB of storage, whereas [Google Maps Go](https://www.anrdoezrs.net/links/3607085/type/dlg/sid/UUhtgUeUpU207252/https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.apps.mapslite) uses a measly 17MB. 270MB might not seem like a lot, but 17MB is barely anything. You'll find the same story across the board.

 What if [storage space](https://screen-mirror.techidaily.com/how-to-mirror-your-samsung-galaxy-s23plus-screen-to-pc-with-chromecast-drfone-by-drfone-android/) is not a huge concern for you? There are other benefits as well. The slimmed-down nature of Go apps means they have fewer features. That's easier on [your battery](https://hardware-updates.techidaily.com/installing-intels-management-engine-drivers-on-windows-11817-complete-tutorial/) and [your data plan](https://win-howtos.techidaily.com/how-to-get-the-brightness-control-back-in-working-order-on-windows-11/) since they're not always trying to do so much. In some cases, the Go apps are faster too, which fewer features bogging down the app.

 In general, Go apps are easier on your phone because that is exactly what they were designed to do. If Gmail Go can work on a phone with less than 2GB of RAM, your [Galaxy S21 FE](https://program-issues.techidaily.com/seamless-gameplay-awaits-resolve-fortnites-launching-glitches-now/) will have resources to spare.

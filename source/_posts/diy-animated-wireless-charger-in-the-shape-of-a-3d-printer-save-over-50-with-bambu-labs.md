Getting started with resin 3D printing is a little more difficult and sometimes expensive than FDM printers such as the[Elegoo Neptune 3 Plus](https://www.tomshardware.com/news/print-big-with-this-black-friday-elegoo-neptune-3-plus-deal) . If you can save a little money, then the overall cost isn't too different to FDM, but resin has a much higher level of detail.

[We reviewed the Anycubic Photo Mono 2](https://www.tomshardware.com/reviews/anycubic-photon-mono-2) and loved the detailed 4K prints, fast setup and effortless print removal.  The build volume of 165 x 89 x 143mm is 20% larger than the photon Mono 4K. This is on the smaller size of resin printers, but our reviewer was able to fit 6 presupported miniatures on the build plate at once.

 Android can make [themes based on the colors from your wallpaper](https://extra-resources.techidaily.com/choosing-a-gimbal-for-smooth-drone-photos-and-videos/). To take it a step further, you can also apply the colors to the icons on your home screen. We'll show you how to get this super custom look.

 "Material You" was introduced in Android 12, and it applies the colors from your wallpaper to things like the Settings, Phone, Messages, and more apps. [Android 13](https://ios-unlock.techidaily.com/how-to-change-country-on-app-store-for-apple-iphone-se-2022-with-7-methods-by-drfone-ios/) builds on that with the ability to theme the home screen icons as well.

 Themed home screen icons are only supported by the [default launcher](https://screen-video-capture.techidaily.com/rapidrush-framefrozen-recording/). For [Samsung Galaxy devices](https://instagram-clips.techidaily.com/new-connecting-consciously-with-content-craftsmen-of-your-realm-for-2024/), that's "One UI Home." Google Pixel phones use the "Pixel Launcher." You'll need to use these in order to see the themed icons.

##  Themed Icons on Samsung Galaxy Phones

 Samsung gives you two options for theming your phone---"Themes" and the "[Color Palette](https://ai-driven-video-production.techidaily.com/split-trim-and-perfect-video-editing-techniques-in-windows-live-movie-maker-for-2024/)." The "Color Palette" is Samsung's implementation of "Material You," and it's what we'll be using.

 First, swipe down once from the top of the screen and tap the gear icon.

![Tap the gear icon.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/2022-08-30_16-26-30.jpg) 

 Next, go to the "Wallpaper and Style" section in the Settings.

![Go to "Wallpaper and Style."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/2022-08-30_16-26-46.jpg)
